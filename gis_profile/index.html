GIS Portfolio - Randall Scharpf

I've been taking a group of classes in Geographic Information Systems (GIS) at UCLA. I've been analyzing geospatial information using a variety of different techniques and creating maps to visualize those analyses.

I've been primarily using ArcGIS Pro as the platform for geographic analysis and rendering in these classes, and have been able to learn a lot of the features of the program by using the ESRI Learning modules. The courses I've completed are visible on my ESRI Learning Transcript.

Sample Maps

  • In my Geography 181A class, we learned to analyze the slope of a section of land. You can take a look at a map of the percent grade of areas within Los Angeles County to see an example of what that looks like.



  • I then used that analysis technique, along with a number of the other techniques we learned in that class, to analyze the actual difficulties of a variety of ski pistes from resorts around the country. As a part of that, I made a map of Bear Mountain Ski Resort from satellite imagery annotated with the locations of each of the ski runs listed on the official resort map. If you're interested in the methods and results of that project, you can read the full report.



  • In my Geography 180 class, I had the chance to look at the median income in every county across the United States by creating this map.



  • For the final assignment of my Geography 180 class, I made an interactive web map of Great Smoky Mountains National Park. You can see the cartographical choices that make the map unique when compared to the official park map on this page.

  • Although all the above maps were generated with ArcGIS Pro, I've also had the chance to work with QGIS, an open source platform for geographic analysis and rendering, my my Geography 7 class. A sample of some work that I produced directly in QGIS for that class can be found here. It takes a look at the amount of water and vegetation in various sections of West LA.
